The Challenge

Undefined User Segments

Hi Echo's user base spans elementary students to adult professionals — each with fundamentally different motivations and pain points. No unified understanding existed.

AI Adoption Uncertainty

The app integrates AI-driven conversation practice, but it was unclear whether users trusted AI feedback, how they used it, and where it fell short of expectations.

Retention Without Insight

Without deep user behavior data, the product team had no reliable signal for why users churned or which features drove long-term engagement.

C + B Dual Market

Individual learners and institutional buyers have distinct needs. The research needed to map both simultaneously and identify where they diverge.

Key Findings

User Behavior Insights
AI Trust Gap
Users accepted AI pronunciation feedback but distrusted AI-generated conversations as "unnatural" — they wanted human-like unpredictability, not scripted exchanges.
Session Behavior
Most sessions lasted under 8 minutes. Users practiced in bursts during commutes or breaks — long-form features were consistently underused.
Motivation Drivers
Exam preparation dominated for students; career advancement drove adult users. The two groups needed fundamentally different content strategies.
Feedback Loops
Users wanted immediate, sentence-level feedback — not aggregate session scores. Delayed reporting felt abstract and disconnected.
Institutional (B-end) Insights
Purchase Trigger
Schools bought based on curriculum alignment and measurable outcomes, not feature lists. Teacher progress reporting was the critical missing piece.
Implementation Barriers
IT complexity and teacher training overhead were the top reasons institutions delayed adoption — onboarding needed a dedicated institutional flow.
Competitive Edge
Hi Echo's AI feedback accuracy rated higher than competitor apps in blind testing, but users didn't know this — a significant perception gap to close.
Retention Pattern
Institutional users who completed teacher onboarding had significantly longer retention cycles — B-end support investment pays off in C-end stickiness.

User Personas

K-12 Learners
Elementary · Middle School
Driven by exam scores and parental pressure. Learning is structured around curriculum, with parents often making the purchase decision.
Uses the app for homework and test prep. Sessions are short and goal-directed.
"I need to pass my oral exam next month — I don't have time for casual practice."
Study-Abroad Test Takers
College Students · Study Abroad Prep
Preparing for IELTS/TOEFL speaking. Higher self-motivation, more willing to explore features deeply.
Values authentic conversation practice over structured drills. Frustrated when AI responses feel formulaic.
"AI feedback is great for pronunciation, but the conversations feel too scripted to be useful."
Career Professionals
Working Professionals
Needs English for meetings, presentations, or international work. Squeezed for time — values high efficiency per minute of practice.
Willing to pay for premium features if ROI is clear and immediate.
"I need to lead a meeting in English next month. I don't need grammar — I need confidence."
05

Product Recommendations

01
Make Speaking Training the Core Product Function
Converge the core loop to: speak -> recognize -> correct -> repeat -> pass. Prioritize ASR accuracy plus pronunciation and fluency feedback, and deliver practice through scenario-based dialogues. Evidence (quant): Among additional learning goals, 88.90% selected improving speaking and 84.19% selected improving listening, making speaking/listening the top priorities. Evidence (qual): Interviewees explicitly said speech recognition accuracy needs improvement.
Priority
02
Launch a Classroom + Teacher Collaboration Mode
Make AI part of the teaching system with a hybrid model: classroom assignments, teacher dashboard, and student practice playback. Teachers assign tasks, students complete practice, and teachers review and comment to close the loop. Evidence (quant): Support for the blended approach scored highest at 4.173/5, above traditional classroom (3.829) and AI-only teaching (3.659), with strongest overall support for traditional + AI together.
Priority
03
Convert Writing-Tool Users into Speaking Users
Build one-click text-to-speaking migration: users paste essays, notes, or key points, and the product generates a spoken script plus shadowing/retelling drills and speaking scores. This lowers speaking activation cost. Evidence (quant): In 1,022 valid surveys, 804 users had heard of non-speaking AI English tools (writing correction/polish), and 641 had used them, showing a large existing base for conversion.
Priority

Outcome & Reflection

Research without execution is just observation. We made sure ours didn't stop at the report.
  • 78-page, 42,000-word report covering C-end (individual learners) and B-end (institutions) — adopted by the NetEase Hi Echo product team with parts landing in product iteration
  • 1,287 surveys across 3 provinces with 85% response rate — covering elementary students to working adults
  • Awarded University First Prize in the "Digital Intelligence Empowerment" track, ranking 2nd out of 18 projects
  • Led a 17-person cross-functional team (writing, technical, design) from research design through final delivery over 9 months
  • Original plan included software development support; pivoted to demo output when technical constraints emerged — maintaining core value while adapting scope pragmatically
